Bio-palindrome - in biology, a palindromic sequence is defined as a nucleic acid sequence that is identical to its reverse complement, also known as a Watson-Crick palindrome or an antipalindrome. Similarly, we consider text strings in which the letter order is identical to that obtained by reading from end to beginning under the Atbash (AZBY) substitution. For example "hovels", "wizard", "milk porn", "wild word", "hoofy bulls" in English and "aviverez", "vivrière" in French. And anyone lucky enough to bear the name Aviv Erez. In Hebrew, bio-palindromic perfect pangrams are also found. This idea was already proposed in the context of balanced words, particularly equidistant words.

When in addition reading from end to beginning without Atbash substitution, gives a second text, possibly after rearranging the positions of spaces, we get an anadromic bio-palindrome.
